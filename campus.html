Campus & Hostel Life

From sunrise to sunset — here's what a day at Amrita Amaravati looks like, and everything you need to settle in comfortably.

A Typical Day at Amrita Amaravati

Your daily routine once classes begin. Get ready for an action-packed day!

6:00 AM — Wake Up & Fresh Up

Most students wake up around 6-7 AM. Fresh up, get ready, and head to the mess for breakfast. Pro tip: Early birds get the best breakfast options!

7:30 AM — Breakfast at Mess

Mess breakfast is served from 7:00 AM to 8:30 AM. Options include idli, dosa, upma, bread, cornflakes, and fruits. Don't skip breakfast — you need the energy!

8:30 AM — Classes Begin

First lecture starts at 8:30 AM. Each class is 50 minutes with a 10-minute break between periods. Labs are usually scheduled in the afternoon.

10:30 AM — Short Break

A quick 10-minute break between periods. Grab a snack from the canteen or chat with friends. The canteen serves tea, coffee, and quick bites.

12:30 PM — Lunch Break

Lunch is served from 12:00 PM to 1:30 PM. The mess serves rice, dal, curry, chapati, and a sweet dish. The menu rotates daily for variety.

1:30 PM — Afternoon Labs / Classes

Post-lunch sessions include lab practicals, project work, or additional theory classes. Labs typically run for 2-3 hours.

4:30 PM — Club Activities / Sports

After classes, head to club meetings, sports practice, library study sessions, or just chill with friends in the common areas.

7:30 PM — Dinner at Mess

Dinner is served from 7:00 PM to 8:30 PM. Similar to lunch with different menu items. After dinner, it's study time or relaxation.

8:30 PM — Study / Project Work

Evening study sessions in the hostel study rooms or library. Many students work on assignments, coding projects, or prepare for upcoming tests.

11:00 PM — Lights Out

Most hostels have a lights-out policy around 11 PM (varies by hostel). Get good sleep — tomorrow is another busy day!

Hostel Facilities

Your home away from home. Here's what you get.

Rooms

Spacious rooms with 2-3 students per room. Each room has individual beds, study tables, chairs, wardrobes, and fans. Some rooms have attached bathrooms.

WiFi & Internet

High-speed campus WiFi available in hostels and academic blocks. The password is provided on your ID card. Speed is good for streaming, coding, and video calls.

Water & Laundry

24/7 drinking water (RO purified) and hot water for bathing. Laundry facilities available with washing machines. Ironing boards are provided too.

Security

24/7 security guards at hostel gates. Biometric entry/exit system. CCTV cameras in common areas. Parents can feel safe about your security.

Study Rooms

Dedicated study rooms in each hostel block for group studies and quiet reading. Open till late night. Perfect for exam preparation.

Medical Facilities

On-campus health center with a doctor and nurse. First-aid kits in hostels. Ambulance available 24/7 for emergencies.

Campus Layout & Important Places

Know your campus like the back of your hand.

Academic Block

Where all your classes happen. Contains lecture halls, seminar rooms, faculty cabins, and department offices. The heart of academics.

Laboratories

State-of-the-art labs for each department. Computer labs, electronics labs, mechanical workshops, and more. Open for project work after class hours.

Library

Well-stocked with textbooks, reference books, journals, and digital resources. Quiet study zones and group discussion rooms available.

Computer Center

High-config computers with all necessary software installed. Open for students to practice coding, work on projects, and access online resources.

Canteen & Cafeteria

Multiple canteens serving snacks, beverages, and quick meals. The go-to place between classes for a quick bite or chai break with friends.

Sports Complex

Cricket ground, football field, basketball court, badminton courts, and indoor games. Hosts all sports events and practice sessions.

Health Center

On-campus medical facility with a qualified doctor and nurse. Open during college hours. Emergency ambulance available 24/7.

Transport & Parking

College buses for day scholars covering major routes. Ample parking for two-wheelers. Bus schedules are shared at the beginning of each semester.

Amma's Shrine / Prayer Hall

A peaceful space for meditation and prayer. Reflects Amrita's spiritual values. Open for all students regardless of faith.

Tips to Settle In During Your First Week

The first week sets the tone for your entire college life. Make it count!

Explore the Campus

Walk around the entire campus on Day 1. Locate your classrooms, labs, library, canteen, and hostel. Knowing your way around saves time and reduces stress.

Meet Your Roommates

Your roommates will be your first friends. Introduce yourself, share your background, and set ground rules for the room. Good roommates = Happy hostel life.

Get Your ID Card ASAP

Your ID card is your lifeline. You need it for mess, library, labs, and campus entry. Get it made on the first day and always carry it.

Stock Up on Essentials

Buy toiletries, stationery, and snacks from the campus store or nearby shops. Keep a small first-aid kit, umbrella, and power bank handy.

Save Important Contacts

Save warden's number, security, health center, and your class representative's contact. Also, share your roommate's number with your parents.

Attend All Orientation Sessions

Orientation might seem boring, but it gives you crucial info about rules, facilities, and opportunities. Plus, it's a great way to meet people!
