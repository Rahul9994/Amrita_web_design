How Classes, Labs & Practicals Work

Understanding the academic structure will help you plan your days better.

Theory Classes

Each subject has 3-4 theory sessions per week (50 minutes each). Classes run from 8:30 AM to 4:30 PM with breaks. Attendance is mandatory and tracked via biometric/ERP system.

Lab Sessions

Practical labs run for 2-3 hours per subject weekly. You must carry your lab manual, observation book, and required equipment. Lab attendance is separate and equally important.

Projects & Mini-Projects

Every semester includes mini-projects or assignments. Final year has a major project. Start thinking about project ideas early — they can make or break your resume!

Attendance Rules

This is NON-NEGOTIABLE. Read carefully.

Minimum Requirement

You MUST maintain a minimum of 75% attendance in every subject (theory + lab combined). Falling below 75% means you are NOT eligible to write the End Semester Exam (ESE) for that subject.

Pro Tip: Aim for 85%+ to be safe. Unexpected leaves happen!

What Happens If You Fall Short?

If your attendance drops below 75%, you get a "Detained" status. This means:

  • You cannot write the ESE for that subject
  • You must re-register for the course next semester/year
  • It delays your graduation and affects your CGPA
  • It looks bad on your academic record

How Attendance is Calculated

Attendance = (Classes Attended / Total Classes Conducted) × 100. Both theory and lab attendance are tracked separately and then combined. Medical leave (with valid certificate) may be considered on a case-by-case basis, but don't rely on it!

Exam Pattern: CIA + ESE

Understanding the grading system is key to acing your academics.

CIA — Continuous Internal Assessment

40% of total marks

  • CIA 1: First internal exam (around Week 6-7)
  • CIA 2: Second internal exam (around Week 12-13)
  • Assignments & Quizzes: Regular assessments throughout
  • Lab Internals: Practical exams and viva
  • Total CIA weightage: 40 marks out of 100

ESE — End Semester Exam

60% of total marks

  • Duration: 3 hours per subject
  • Pattern: Mix of MCQs, short answers, and long answers
  • Weightage: 60 marks out of 100
  • Passing: Minimum 40% in ESE + overall 50%
  • Supplementary: Re-exam if you fail (try to avoid this!)

Tips to Maintain a Good CGPA from Day 1

Follow these golden rules and you'll be among the toppers.

Attend Every Class

It sounds obvious, but skipping classes is the #1 reason students struggle. Professors often share exam hints and important topics only in class.

Take Good Notes

Write notes in your own words. Use diagrams and flowcharts. Review them weekly, not just before exams. Good notes = Easy revision.

Form Study Groups

Study with 2-3 friends from your branch. Explain concepts to each other. Teaching is the best way to learn. Plus, group studies are more fun!

Practice Coding Daily

If you're in CSE/CSE-AI/CSE-DS, code every single day. Use LeetCode, HackerRank, or GeeksforGeeks. 30 minutes daily is better than 5 hours before exams.

Start Assignments Early

Don't procrastinate. Start assignments the day they're given. Last-minute submissions are stressful and usually lower quality.

Use NPTEL & Online Resources

Amrita has tie-ups with NPTEL. Watch video lectures, solve previous year papers, and use online platforms to strengthen weak topics.
